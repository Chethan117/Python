. Transaction Authorization Categories:
Approved:

The transaction is successfully authorized, and the payment can proceed.
Example: Customer has sufficient funds, and all security checks pass.
Referred:

The transaction is neither approved nor declined immediately. It requires manual intervention or further verification by the bank or card issuer.
Example: Suspicious activity detected, or additional checks are needed before approval.
Pickup:

The transaction is declined, and the card is marked for confiscation. This usually happens when the card is reported stolen or thereâ€™s suspected fraud.
Example: Merchant is instructed to keep the card if presented physically.
Declined:

The transaction is rejected due to various reasons like insufficient funds, incorrect card details, or security blocks.
